After months of alarmed preparation, China's leadership has begun to see Donald J. Trump's return to the presidency as an opportunity to reset relations with Washington, according to officials and analysts familiar with Beijing's thinking.

The shift reflects both China's experience navigating Mr. Trump's first term and its confidence that it can manage the economic disruptions that his proposed tariffs might bring. Beijing now believes that Mr. Trump, despite his combative rhetoric, is ultimately a transactional leader who can be negotiated with.

"They've concluded that Trump is someone you can do business with," said one analyst who advises the Chinese government. "That wasn't the view four years ago."

The change in perspective comes as China's economy has grown more self-sufficient and as Mr. Trump has surrounded himself with advisers who favor a deal with Beijing over a prolonged confrontation. Chinese officials have quietly signaled that they are prepared to offer concessions on trade, technology and other issues in exchange for a reduction in tariffs.
