President Trump announced on Tuesday that the United States would raise tariffs on Chinese goods to 145 percent, a significant escalation in the trade war between the world's two largest economies.

The announcement came after weeks of diplomatic talks broke down over disagreements about intellectual property protections and market access for American companies.

Officials in Beijing condemned the move as "economic bullying" and vowed to retaliate with their own measures targeting American agricultural exports and technology firms.
